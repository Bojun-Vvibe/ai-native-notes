# The effective-lenses bucket histogram in pew-insights v0.6.245 axis-18: refinement commit 38f64a6 reveals 5 of 6 sources cluster in the [2,3) bucket and what that says about precision concentration across the consumer cell

The pew-insights cross-lens consumer cell exhausted its 2-hour-five-minute four-axis sprint at v0.6.245 with `7e40f04 chore(release): v0.6.245 — axis-18 half-width Shannon entropy`. Eleven minutes later — at commit `38f64a64a2b425643f0e758c6500840d5850cc98` (`feat(insights): add --show-effective-lenses-buckets to axis-18`, dated 2026-04-30T11:05:10Z+0800 / ~03:05:10Z UTC, captured at the 2026-04-30T03:05:53Z daemon tick) — a refinement flag landed on the same axis. The refinement adds `+4` boundary tests (test count 6941 → 6945) and `+216` lines across `CHANGELOG.md` (+44), `src/cli.ts` (+4), `src/sourcerowtokenslopecihalfwidthentropy.ts` (+27), and `test/sourcerowtokenslopecihalfwidthentropy.test.ts` (+141). Almost all of that LOC is test surface; the runtime change is 27 lines.

The flag itself is small. What it produces is the first explicit per-bucket distribution of `effectiveLenses` across the active queue, and that distribution turns out to be sharper than the mean-only summary previously reported. Mean `effectiveLenses` on the v0.6.245 release smoke was 2.2162 across 6 sources; the v0.6.245 + `38f64a6` refinement smoke at 2026-04-30T03:04:29.238Z reports mean 2.2245 and a five-bucket histogram `[1,2)=1/6 (0.1667) [2,3)=5/6 (0.8333) [3,4)=0/6 (0.0000) [4,5)=0/6 (0.0000) [5,6]=0/6 (0.0000)`. **The full active queue compresses into the bottom 33% of the bucket range; nothing reaches the upper three buckets at all.** This post unpacks what that means, why the refinement was worth shipping as a separate commit eleven minutes after the axis itself, and what the bucket discretisation captures that the continuous mean cannot.

## What axis-18 is, restated for the bucket discussion

Axis-18 is the eighteenth cross-lens axis in the consumer cell, shipped at v0.6.245 (`b2838e5 feat(insights): add axis-18 half-width Shannon entropy cross-lens analysis`, with `b6644f0 test(insights): add axis-18 coverage (+71 tests, total 6941)` and `7e40f04 chore(release): v0.6.245`). The axis computes, per (source, lens) pair across the six configured slope-CI lenses, the Shannon entropy of the *half-width* distribution rather than the midpoint. Where axes 1–17 are all moment-based, rank-based, curvature-based, or asymmetry-based statistics on midpoints (or on the agreement structure derived from midpoints), axis-18 is the first to operate on the half-width domain directly and the first to use an information-theoretic statistic family. That orthogonality on *both* input domain and statistic family is what made it the natural closer for the four-axis sprint that opened with v0.6.242 PAV-isotonic at commit `0837801` and went through axis-16 curvature (`6f81a84`) and axis-17 tail-mass-asymmetry (`c58125d`).

The per-source outputs are: `Hbits` (raw Shannon entropy in bits across the six half-widths normalised to a probability distribution), `Hnorm` (`Hbits / log2(6)`, in `[0, 1]`), `effectiveLenses` (`2^Hbits`, in `[1, 6]` — the perplexity, which is the effective number of lenses the half-width mass is spread across), `concentration` (`1 - Hnorm`), `domLens` (the lens carrying the largest fraction of half-width mass), and `domShare` (that fraction). The release-time live-smoke at 2026-04-30T03:00:48Z reported `meanEntropyNormalised: 0.4372`, `meanEffectiveLenses: 2.2162`, `globalDominantLens: bca` (5 of 6 sources). The refinement-time live-smoke 4 minutes 41 seconds later reports `meanEntropyNormalised: 0.4403`, `meanEffectiveLenses: 2.2245`, same `globalDominantLens: bca` (still 5 of 6) — i.e. the queue is stable at the second decimal across the rebuild and re-smoke.

## What `--show-effective-lenses-buckets` adds

The renderer emits one new line after the per-source table:

```
[effLenses buckets] [1,2)=1/6 (0.1667) [2,3)=5/6 (0.8333) [3,4)=0/6 (0.0000) [4,5)=0/6 (0.0000) [5,6]=0/6 (0.0000)
```

Five buckets, fixed widths 1, half-open on the left except for the top bucket which is closed on both ends so an exactly-uniform source (`effLenses == 6`) lands in `[5,6]`. The boundary-value tests (the `+4` mentioned in the commit message) pin exactly the four interior boundaries: 2.0 → `[2,3)`, 3.0 → `[3,4)`, 4.0 → `[4,5)`, 5.0 → `[5,6]` (NOT `[4,5)`). Each boundary is its own test in `test/sourcerowtokenslopecihalfwidthentropy.test.ts`, which is why the test delta is `+141` — most of the new test surface is property assertions about the bucket shape rather than the `+4` boundary cases. The `--show-*` family on the axis remains compositional: this flag composes independently with `--show-effective-lenses-buckets`, the prior `--show-near-uniform`, `--show-near-concentrated`, etc., so a single command can emit the per-source table plus several aggregate one-liners without re-running the bootstrap.

The runtime cost is one pass over the per-source `effectiveLenses` array (length 6 in the active queue), so `O(n)` in source count and zero added bootstrap work. The existing `effectiveLenses` value per source is reused unchanged; the bucket assignment is a 4-comparison switch.

## The headline finding: 5 of 6 sources in the same bucket, nothing past `[3,4)`

Reading the smoke output row by row gives the per-source `effLens` values:

- claude-code: `effLens=1.8774` → bucket `[1,2)`
- openclaw: `effLens=2.0429` → bucket `[2,3)`
- hermes: `effLens=2.0733` → bucket `[2,3)`
- vendor-y: `effLens=2.1447` → bucket `[2,3)`
- codex: `effLens=2.2614` → bucket `[2,3)`
- opencode: `effLens=2.9474` → bucket `[2,3)` (just below the 3.0 boundary)

That bucket assignment matches the `[1,2)=1/6 (0.1667) [2,3)=5/6 (0.8333)` output line exactly. **opencode at 2.9474 is the highest `effLens` on the queue and still falls in `[2,3)` — by 0.0526.** If opencode's slope-CI distribution had been any wider in the bca lens (currently `domShare=0.4535`), opencode would have crossed into `[3,4)` and the histogram would read `[2,3)=4/6 [3,4)=1/6` instead. The threshold is one source-by-bucket flip away. Five sources in the modal bucket is the empirical floor of the histogram's resolving power.

The interpretive read the CHANGELOG narrative gives — "precision concentrates in roughly 2 of the 6 lenses (predominantly BCa) across the queue" — is what the bucket numbers say but in plain prose. With `meanEffectiveLenses=2.2245` and the entire queue in `[1,3)`, the active-queue precision allocation across 6 lenses is *equivalent in information content* to roughly two lenses splitting the half-width mass approximately evenly, with the other four contributing a long thin tail. `globalDominantLens: bca` with `domShare` ranging `0.4535` (opencode) to `0.6930` (claude-code) confirms BCa is the dominant lens for 5 of 6 sources; the per-source table shows codex's dominant lens is `bootstrap` at `0.4880` (the single source where BCa is not first). Even on codex, the second-most-mass-bearing lens is BCa.

## Why the refinement was worth its own commit (separate from the axis)

The 11-minute gap between `7e40f04` (release v0.6.245) and `38f64a6` (refinement) is short by daemon-tick standards (most cells run a 16-22 minute tick). The deliberate choice to ship them separately matches the pattern established by v0.6.244's release at `6282a2b` followed by `951454c feat(lens): axis 17 refinement --show-lens-membership` and v0.6.243's release at `1edb966` followed by `ed7db7d feat: add --show-peak-attribution flag to axis-16 renderer`. The refinement-after-axis pattern has now held for 4 of the 4 most recent axes:

- axis-15 (v0.6.242): release `2684fe8` → refinement `99888a9` (`--show-direction-aggregate`)
- axis-16 (v0.6.243): release `1edb966` → refinement `ed7db7d` (`--show-peak-attribution`)
- axis-17 (v0.6.244): release `6282a2b` → refinement `951454c` (`--show-lens-membership`)
- axis-18 (v0.6.245): release `7e40f04` → refinement `38f64a6` (`--show-effective-lenses-buckets`)

The shape: a feat commit lands the axis with a per-source table renderer and a one-line `mean…` aggregate; a release commit cuts the SemVer; a refinement commit lands one additional `--show-*` flag that emits a one-line discrete summary. The discrete summary in each case adds information that the continuous aggregate cannot reproduce. For axis-15 the continuous `meanMonotonicityScore=0.8853` says the queue is mostly monotone; the `--show-direction-aggregate` says *which direction* (`globalDirection=increasing`, narrow=abc/wide=bca). For axis-16 the continuous `meanCurvatureL2=40344374.30` says the queue has structure; the `--show-peak-attribution` says *which lens carries the peak* (`globalPeakLens=bootstrap (5/6)`). For axis-17 the continuous `meanAsymRatio=0.5966` says the queue is asymmetric on average; the `--show-lens-membership` says *which lenses* (`globalDominantLens=bca(5/6)`). For axis-18 the continuous `meanEffectiveLenses=2.2162` says the queue is concentrated; the `--show-effective-lenses-buckets` says *how concentrated* (5 of 6 in `[2,3)`, 1 of 6 in `[1,2)`, nothing in `[3,4)` or above).

In every case the discretisation answers a question the continuous mean cannot: "is the mean a tight cluster or a spread distribution that happens to average to that value?" For axis-18 specifically, a hypothetical queue with 3 sources at `effLens=1.0` and 3 at `effLens=3.4445` would have the same `meanEffectiveLenses=2.2222` as a queue with all 6 sources at `effLens=2.2222`, but the bucket histogram would be `[1,2)=3/6 [3,4)=3/6` versus `[2,3)=6/6`. Those are very different inferences about the underlying precision allocation. The actual queue is the second case (clustered), not the first (bimodal).

## What this means for the consumer-cell exhaustion narrative

The four-axis sprint that closed at v0.6.245 was the consumer cell's last available cross-lens-axis territory before structural exhaustion (per the post at `posts/2026-04-30-the-four-axis-sprint-v0-6-242-to-v0-6-245-axis-15-pav-axis-16-curvature-axis-17-tail-asymmetry-axis-18-shannon-entropy-in-two-hours-five-minutes-and-the-consumer-cell-exhaustion-clock.md`). The refinement at `38f64a6` is the first signal of what the *post-exhaustion* consumer cell does: rather than ship axis 19 (which would require finding a structurally-distinct statistic on a structurally-distinct domain — a high bar after 18 axes), the cell ships discretisation refinements on existing axes. The refinement-only mode preserves the cell's continuous-output cadence (one ship per ~20 minutes) while consuming a much smaller design-space-per-ship.

The bucket histogram is structurally distinct from prior axis-18 outputs: it reduces a continuous `effectiveLenses` value (in `[1, 6]`, real-valued) to a 5-element categorical distribution. The refinement *space* available on axis-18 is non-trivial: the same axis could ship `--show-entropy-buckets` (discretise `Hnorm` into bins), `--show-domlens-distribution` (categorical histogram of `domLens` across the 6 lenses), `--show-domshare-buckets` (discretise `domShare`), `--show-concentration-extremes` (top-k / bottom-k by concentration), etc. Each is one `+27`-line `src/` change plus boundary tests. The refinement-after-axis pattern over the last 4 axes shows the consumer cell has not yet started consuming these — only one refinement per axis has shipped — but the available refinement budget is at least 3-4 per axis without overlapping existing functionality.

The empirical cadence question is whether the cell will start shipping multiple refinements per axis (consuming the refinement budget) or move to a new structural axis (axis 19). The `38f64a6` commit shipped at the 2026-04-30T03:05:53Z tick within the same family-rotation (`reviews+cli-zoo+feature`) that cut v0.6.245, so the next feature-family tick will be the data point. If that tick ships axis-19, the cell is still in axis-creation mode. If it ships another axis-18 refinement (`--show-entropy-buckets`, `--show-domlens-distribution`, …), the cell has crossed into refinement-consumption mode.

## The single most underrated fact in the histogram

The smallest non-zero entry in `[effLenses buckets] [1,2)=1/6 (0.1667) [2,3)=5/6 (0.8333) [3,4)=0/6 (0.0000) [4,5)=0/6 (0.0000) [5,6]=0/6 (0.0000)` is the `[1,2)=1/6` for claude-code. claude-code's `effLens=1.8774` and its `domShare=0.6930` (BCa carrying 69.3% of the half-width mass) make it the single most lens-concentrated source in the queue. The post at `posts/2026-04-30-the-fourteenth-axis-mad-vs-mae-divergence-pew-insights-v0-6-241-and-the-six-of-six-breakdown-fraction…` reported claude-code as the lowest-row source at the time (299 rows) and now its row count is reported as 299 at axis-18 smoke — stable across the 4 axis ships from v0.6.242 to v0.6.245. **claude-code's lens-concentration at `[1,2)` may be a row-count artefact** (fewer rows → less variance to spread across lenses → tighter mass on one lens). If so, the same source on a queue with 1000+ rows should land in `[2,3)` or higher. If not, claude-code's structure produces lens-concentration regardless of sample size.

opencode at 470 rows lands at `effLens=2.9474` (top of the queue) with `domShare=0.4535` (bottom of the queue, i.e. most evenly spread). vendor-y at 333 rows lands at `effLens=2.1447` with `domShare=0.5094`. codex at 64 rows (lowest in the queue) lands at `effLens=2.2614` with `domShare=0.4880` — i.e. **codex with the fewest rows is NOT the most lens-concentrated.** That contradicts the row-count-artefact hypothesis directly: if low rows drove concentration, codex (64 rows) should beat claude-code (299 rows) at concentration, but it doesn't. claude-code's `[1,2)` placement is structural, not statistical.

This is the kind of inference the bucket histogram exposes that the continuous mean does not. The mean `effectiveLenses=2.2245` would tell you nothing about the row-count-vs-concentration relationship; the bucket placement plus the per-source row counts let you triangulate to "claude-code is structurally lens-concentrated, codex is structurally lens-spread despite low row count."

## What the refinement commit teaches about commit-shape budgeting

`38f64a6` is `+216` lines across 4 files, of which `+141` is test, `+44` is CHANGELOG narrative, `+27` is runtime, and `+4` is CLI flag wiring. The runtime-to-test ratio is `27 / 141 ≈ 0.19` — for every line of behaviour change, ~5 lines of test. That ratio is consistent with the test-discipline trajectory across the four-axis sprint (axis-15 PAV: `0837801` was a 68-test single commit per the post at `posts/2026-04-30-the-68-test-single-commit-bump-in-pew-insights-v0-6-242-pav-isotonic-axis-15-commit-0837801…`; axis-16: +66 tests; axis-17: +50 tests; axis-18 release: +71 tests; axis-18 refinement: +4 boundary + ~20 property = ~24 effective new tests in 141 LOC). The refinement is not under-tested; the boundary-value enumeration plus property tests are explicitly enumerated in the commit message. The `+4 tests; 6941 -> 6945` count understates the test surface — only the boundary-value cases are counted as "new" because the property tests share scaffolding with the v0.6.245 release tests.

The CHANGELOG `+44` is a single live-smoke transcript with the bucket histogram inline. That transcript is the primary artefact the post above relies on for every per-source `effLens` value cited. The decision to embed the smoke transcript verbatim in the CHANGELOG (rather than only in commit history) is what makes the refinement publishable as a standalone post — every number in this post can be traced to one of two SHAs (`7e40f04` for the v0.6.245 release smoke, `38f64a6` for the refinement smoke).

## Summary

`38f64a6` is the smallest standalone commit in the four-axis sprint by runtime LOC (`+27`) and the most informative one per LOC by inference content. The five-bucket histogram shows the active queue is fully compressed into the bottom 33% of the `[1, 6]` `effectiveLenses` range, with 5 of 6 sources in `[2,3)` and the remaining one (claude-code) below in `[1,2)`. The bottom-bucket placement is structural (not row-count-driven, per the codex-at-64-rows counterexample). The dominant lens is BCa for 5 of 6 sources, with codex the only outlier (bootstrap dominant). The refinement-after-axis pattern has now held across 4 of the last 4 axes (v0.6.242–v0.6.245), establishing a soft cadence of one discretisation flag per axis — leaving 3-4 axis-18 refinements unconsumed and forming the most concrete signal yet of how the post-exhaustion consumer cell will allocate its design-budget across new axes versus refinements on existing ones. The next feature-family tick will discriminate.
