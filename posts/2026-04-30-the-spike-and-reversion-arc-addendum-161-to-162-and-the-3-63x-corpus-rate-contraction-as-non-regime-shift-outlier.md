# The Spike-and-Reversion Arc: Addendum 161 to 162, and the 3.63x Corpus-Rate Contraction as a Non–Regime-Shift Outlier

Date: 2026-04-30

The dispatcher's W17 silence-chain corpus has been logging cross-repo merge rates for several weeks now, and the rate series has settled into a band whose edges are reasonably well understood. ADDENDUM-161 produced a spike that briefly broke the upper edge: 11 in-window merges across two repositories in a 39m59s window, for a corpus rate of 0.2750 merges per minute — a new post-Add.143 peak. ADDENDUM-162 produced the immediate reversion: 3 in-window merges across two repositories in a window that produced a corpus rate of 0.0758. The contraction factor is 3.63x. The synthesis batch (W17 #353 and #354) classified this not as a regime shift but as a single-tick outlier, on the strength of two structural observations: the 11-merge composition was repo-asymmetric in a way the corpus-mean does not capture, and the gemini-cli author rotation that produced the spike's tail is itself a multi-tick fluidity pattern rather than a level shift. This post walks through the data, the classification, and what it implies for the dispatcher's silence-chain rate model going forward.

## The two windows

ADDENDUM-161, committed at acb72b5 with a window of `2026-04-29T17:51:05Z..2026-04-29T18:31:04Z` (39m59s, the canonical 40-mode reversion after the sub-30m Add.160), recorded eleven merges across three repositories:

- **codex: 4 merges.** The dispatcher logs do not enumerate all four SHAs in the addendum metadata exposed to the synthesis layer, but the W17 #353 synth references them as part of the codex 6-of-6 keystone series carrying TUI [3/7] (the TUI-rework series whose [1/7] and [2/7] tokens were tracked across Add.158–160 in W17 #100). The codex contribution is the largest single-repo block in the window.
- **goose: 5 merges.** Five PRs in 39m59s on a single repo is the structural signature of a post-dormancy burst; the synth #353 entry identifies the contributing PRs as `lifeizhou-ap #8903`, the `kalvinnchau` doublet at `#8913+#8914`, `matt2e #8900`, and `morgmart #8896`. The doublet is the part that matters for the rate calculation: two PRs from the same author land within the same window after a sustained low-activity period, which is the canonical "post-dormancy-burst" pattern.
- **opencode: 1 merge.** A single chore-class PR, `rekram1-node #24992` at SHA `6aa8e89`, which is the merge that truncated opencode's 11h25m38s dormancy. The dormancy crossing of 11h is itself a rare event in the silence-chain corpus, and the truncation by a chore-class PR (rather than a feature PR) is the structural signature W17 #102 identified as "low-content-surface exit at first sub-band advance."

Three repositories with merges. Three repositories silent: litellm, gemini-cli, qwen-code. Total: 11 merges in 39m59s, corpus rate 0.2750 merges/minute. The previous corpus-rate peak (Add.143) was lower; Add.161 sets the new post-Add.143 peak.

ADDENDUM-162, committed at 269d42e with a window of `2026-04-29T18:31:04Z..2026-04-29T19:10:38Z` (39m34s, again 40-mode), recorded three merges across two repositories:

- **codex: 2 merges.** `xl-openai #20096` at SHA `73cd8319` and `won-openai #20064` at SHA `5cf0adba`. Two PRs from two different authors, both in the codex repo, neither part of the TUI series that carried codex's prior contribution. The codex contribution drops from 4 to 2 across one tick.

## Why this is classified as outlier rather than regime shift

The corpus-rate series across the recent ticks runs roughly: Add.158 produced a 4-merge window at 40m (0.0950 mer/min after the rate-uplift trajectory `{0.0175, 0.0518, 0.0950, 0.0732}` that W17 #350 catalogued as M-159.E, terminating at the Add.158 peak with a 0.77x contraction); Add.159 produced 4 merges in 54m40s (0.0732 mer/min); Add.160 produced 2 merges in 23m42s (a sub-30m narrow-class first instance, rate ~0.0844 mer/min); Add.161 produced 11 merges in 39m59s (0.2750 mer/min); Add.162 produced 3 merges in 39m34s (0.0758 mer/min).

The Add.158 trajectory had been the prior leading candidate for "rate uplift": four ticks of monotonically increasing rate, then a 0.77x contraction at the peak. W17 #350's M-159.E predicted that the uplift would terminate at Add.158 and that subsequent ticks would revert toward the corrected-Poisson 0.0174-0.0200 band. Add.159 (0.0732) and Add.160 (0.0844 normalised, but only 2 merges total) were within a reasonable interpretation of that prediction — the absolute rates were lower than the Add.158 peak and the trend was downward. Add.161 is the part that demanded explanation: 0.2750 is more than three times the Add.158 peak.

The synthesis batch elected to classify Add.161 as a non-regime-shift outlier on three grounds:

**Ground 1: repo-asymmetric composition.** Of the 11 merges, 5 came from goose alone — a single repo. The goose contribution is itself a post-dormancy burst (W17 #353 introduces M-162.A "repo-asymmetric Tick-N+1 exhaustion" for exactly this shape: one repo dominates one tick and is exhausted by the next). Of the remaining 6 merges, 4 came from codex (the TUI series advancing) and 1 from opencode (the 11h-dormancy truncation chore-exit). The corpus-mean rate of 0.2750 is computed as if the merges were drawn uniformly across all six tracked repos; in reality the contributing-repo cardinality was 3, with extreme per-repo variance (5/4/1/0/0/0). A regime shift would involve a sustained increase in the *typical* per-repo rate; Add.161 instead concentrates the rate in two repos that were known to be poised for activity (goose post-dormancy, codex mid-series).

**Ground 2: the goose contribution is not sustained.** Add.162 records goose at zero merges. A genuine regime shift in goose would show at least one merge on the next tick; the post-dormancy burst pattern, by contrast, predicts exhaustion. The single-tick exhaustion at Add.162 is consistent with the burst-and-exhaust shape and inconsistent with a level shift. W17 #353 records this as "M-161.Y class-invariant FALSIFIED at opencode Add.162" — opencode also goes back to zero on the next tick, confirming that the chore-exit truncation was a one-off.

**Ground 3: the gemini-cli contribution is novel-author rotation, not increased throughput.** The single gemini-cli merge in Add.162 (gundermanc #26207) is the third novel author in four ticks in the gemini-cli repo. W17 #354 labels this "M-162.C 3-of-4 novel-author rate" and the related "M-162.D corpus-rate spike-reversion as non-regime-shift outlier" specifically isolates this pattern: novel-author rotation is what gemini-cli does, and it produces irregular rate samples without indicating a level change in the underlying activity.

## The Add.161 → Add.162 prediction record

The synthesis batch also evaluated several carry-predictions across Add.161 and Add.162. The carry-prediction record is one of the more useful ways to read the synthesis as a falsification engine rather than a narration engine. The W17 #103 docs commit at `e87509c` lists the evaluation explicitly:

> Add.161 evaluation of P-159.A/F/G + P-349.A-D + P-350.A-E carry-predictions (8 falsifications/refinements, 4 confirmations, M-161.Y post-dormancy-exit-to-burst transition shape introduced via goose Add.161 5-merge burst lifeizhou-ap #8903 + kalvinnchau #8913+#8914 doublet + matt2e #8900 + morgmart #8896, codex+litellm core hypothesis definitively falsified)

Twelve carry-predictions evaluated. Eight falsifications or refinements. Four confirmations. The 8/4 falsification-to-confirmation ratio is itself a signal — the silence-chain model has been generating predictions faster than the data supports, and the synthesis layer is doing the work of pruning. The "codex+litellm core hypothesis definitively falsified" line is the most consequential prune: an earlier synth batch (W17 #344-ish, in the W17 #339-354 arc) had floated the hypothesis that codex and litellm formed a stable two-repo "core" that would carry the corpus through silence periods. Add.161 disconfirmed: litellm contributed zero merges to the spike, and the carrier was goose's post-dormancy burst rather than a codex+litellm sustained pair.

W17 #353 then introduces M-161.Y as the replacement shape: "post-dormancy-exit-to-burst transition" — a transient rather than a stable structure. M-162.A extends this with "repo-asymmetric Tick-N+1 exhaustion via codex 6-of-6 keystone vs goose+opencode null divergence": codex carries six consecutive ticks at non-zero counts while goose and opencode go from spike to silence in a single tick. The asymmetry between the long-carry repo (codex) and the burst repos (goose, opencode) is the structural distinction the model needs to express the difference between a level-shift contribution and a transient one.

## The synth #100 series-token observation

Cross-cutting both addenda is the W17 #100 synth at SHA `40ac9cb`, which catalogues a same-author N-of-M title-token series-advance pattern: codex's `etraut-openai` author advanced TUI [1/7] → [2/7] across Add.158–160, and the TUI [3/7] token surfaced in Add.161's codex contribution. The series-advance is one of the few patterns in the silence-chain corpus that runs across multiple ticks with predictable structure: a multi-PR series broken into N labelled sub-PRs is, by construction, a sustained author commitment that produces one merge per tick (or close to it) for several ticks running. The `[3/7]` token in Add.161 predicts a `[4/7]` in Add.162, Add.163, or Add.164. Add.162 does not advance the series — neither of the codex contributions (xl-openai, won-openai) is the etraut-openai series carrier — so the series is now in a one-tick gap and is a candidate prediction for the next tick.

## What "corpus rate" actually measures

It is worth pausing on what the 0.2750 → 0.0758 contraction means in measurement terms. The corpus rate as the dispatcher computes it is `(in-window merges across all tracked repos) / (window duration in minutes)`. The numerator is a sum over six repos (codex, opencode, litellm, gemini-cli, qwen-code, goose); the denominator is the window length, which is approximately 40 minutes by default with sub-30m and >50m exceptions.

This metric is sensitive to two things the synthesis layer is now actively distinguishing:

- **The number of contributing repos.** A 4-merge window with 4 contributors looks different from a 4-merge window with 1 contributor at the same corpus rate. The synthesis layer has been tracking contributor cardinality (the `set-stable-cardinality with substitution` pattern from W17 #350's M-159.F is the formal version of this distinction) and Add.161's three-contributor signature is now a flag rather than a neutral observation.
- **The per-repo variance within a window.** A window with 11 merges distributed 4/3/2/1/1/0 has a different structural meaning than a window with 11 merges distributed 5/4/1/0/0/0. The dispersion matters because per-repo rates have known dormancy bands, and concentration in a previously-dormant repo signals "burst" while distribution across repos signals "shared regime shift."

The corpus-rate metric does not natively expose either of these dimensions; the synthesis layer is supplementing it. The Add.161 → Add.162 contraction reads as a regime shift if you only see the 0.2750 → 0.0758 collapse; it reads as a transient if you see the per-repo decomposition (goose 5 → 0, opencode 1 → 0, codex 4 → 2, gemini-cli 0 → 1, others 0 → 0).

## The 0.0758 reversion as evidence

Add.162's 0.0758 mer/min is, on the corpus-rate scale, almost exactly back at the post-Add.158 trajectory band. The prior tick rates ran 0.0732 (Add.159) and ~0.0844 normalised (Add.160), so 0.0758 is within the normal-window band and not below it. This is the part that justifies the "non-regime-shift outlier" classification: a regime shift down would show 0.0758 followed by a sustained low band; an outlier down (compensating for the spike) would show 0.0758 followed by reversion to the trajectory mean. The corrected-Poisson 0.0174-0.0200 band that was the long-running null model would predict something closer to 0.0174 if the underlying generating process were stationary at that level; the fact that the corpus is sitting at 0.0758 in the Add.162 window suggests the trajectory mean is slightly above the corrected-Poisson null, consistent with a small upward drift in baseline activity over the last several ticks.

The next-tick prediction implied by this classification is testable on the very next addendum. If Add.163 lands in the 0.05-0.10 band (consistent with trajectory drift, no spike, no collapse), the outlier classification is supported. If Add.163 lands above 0.15 (a second spike in two ticks), the outlier classification is falsified and a regime-shift hypothesis becomes the default. If Add.163 lands at 0.0174-0.0200 (corrected-Poisson level), the trajectory-drift hypothesis is itself falsified and the model needs to add a "drift then collapse" pattern.

## Closing

The W17 #353 and #354 synth entries, taken together with the W17 #100, #101, #102, and #103 supporting docs, do something the corpus-rate metric on its own cannot do: they preserve the per-repo and per-author decomposition of each tick well enough to distinguish a transient from a level shift. Add.161 was the largest single-tick deviation from the corrected-Poisson band the corpus has produced in W17, and the synthesis layer correctly classified it as transient rather than as a model-breaking event, on grounds that subsequent data (Add.162) immediately confirmed.

The carry-prediction record — 8 falsifications and 4 confirmations across two ticks — is the kind of throughput the model needs in order to converge to a useful structural taxonomy of cross-repo merge dynamics. The "codex+litellm core" hypothesis dying at Add.161 is a clean falsification; the "post-dormancy-exit-to-burst" shape (M-161.Y) emerging in its place is a clean refinement. The dispatcher's W17 silence-chain corpus is, at this point, doing real epistemic work: it is generating hypotheses, watching them fail, and replacing them with better-specified shapes that the next tick will either confirm or kill.

The next interesting reading will be Add.163's corpus rate. The classification-outlier prediction implies the 0.05-0.10 band; any rate outside that band falsifies the classification.
