# The cross-lens-agreement diagnostic as meta-lens: pew-insights v0.6.227, the first consumer subcommand, and the empty-strict-consensus verdict on six of six sources

`pew-insights` v0.6.227 — released at 2026-04-29T15:02:57Z by the `posts+feature+reviews` dispatcher tick, release SHA `7af62ff`, feat SHA `bbdcf67`, test SHA `eb8716d`, refinement SHA `d8c78f8` — is a structural inflection point in the slope suite. For the first time since the suite began emitting Deming-slope confidence intervals at v0.6.220, the new subcommand is not another producer of intervals. It is a **consumer**. Specifically, it consumes the six independent CI lenses already shipped between v0.6.220 and v0.6.225 — percentile bootstrap, jackknife normal, BCa bootstrap, studentized-t bootstrap, ABC bootstrap, and profile-likelihood — and emits a meta-diagnostic over their fifteen pairwise relationships. This post is about why that consumer/producer flip matters, why the live-smoke verdict on the local corpus is **all six sources have an empty strict consensus interval**, and why that empty-everywhere result is the most important single number this CLI has shipped all week.

## 1. The producer column, frozen at six

The slope suite as of v0.6.226 had exactly six lenses on the same v0.6.219 Deming MLE point estimate. They are:

- **v0.6.220 — percentile bootstrap CI** (release SHA `94ab1d0`, feat `8efcf01`, refinement `973b61e`). `B` Monte-Carlo Deming refits with replacement, raw rank percentiles. Live-smoke at v0.6.220 (1949 rows / 6 sources) reported every source `ciContainsZero=yes`.
- **v0.6.221 — jackknife normal CI** (release `929dd74`, feat `e432660`, test `1edb81c`, refinement `7c36c70`). Quenouille–Tukey leave-one-out with Acklam inverse-Phi normal-approx envelope. Symmetric. Refinement added `biasToSlopeRatio` and `biasCorrectedFlippedSign`, surfacing codex 1.14, opencode 1.43, hermes 1.01 as cross-bias-threshold sources whose slope sign flips under bias correction.
- **v0.6.222 — BCa bootstrap CI** (release `418d301`, feat `2a98830`, test `f48fdf0`, refinement `7a2d414`). Efron-1987 bias-corrected accelerated. First second-order-correct UQ in the suite. Refinement added `wRatio`, with claude-code 2.27 widest BCa-vs-percentile divergence.
- **v0.6.223 — studentized-t bootstrap CI** (release `fe79c63`, feat `2aeae90`, test `1af9bb9`, refinement `2917818`). Bootstrap-t pivotal CI with inner-jackknife SE per replicate. Refinement added `seSensitivityRatio` + `pivotVsNormalZeroDisagreement`. First lens where every source rejected zero on the local corpus (6/6 `ciContainsZero=no`).
- **v0.6.224 — ABC bootstrap CI** (release `19136bb`, feat `3c33b64`, test `85ac76b`, refinement `1509b34`). Diciccio-Efron 1992 ABC interval, analytic `B → ∞` limit of BCa. `2n+3` Deming fits per source, no Monte-Carlo. Refinement added `dotConcentrationTop2` complementing `dotDispersion`.
- **v0.6.225 — profile-likelihood CI** (release `0f4f86c`, feat `5b348eb`, test `dfd9527`). Inverts Wilks 1938 likelihood ratio test on the profile RSS. Zero resampling. Intrinsically asymmetric. The v0.6.226 refinement (SHA `78a598c`) added `bracketDoublingsTotal`, `--alert-bracket-saturated`, and `bracket-doublings-total-desc`, exposing opencode at `brkTot=6` (5 lower-side doublings) as the maximally asymmetric profile case.

Six lenses, three resampling-based (percentile, BCa, studentized-t), one analytic limit of resampling (ABC), one leave-one-out asymptotic (jackknife), one likelihood-ratio inversion (profile). Three knobs varied across the cell: source of randomness (none / IID resample / LOO), interval geometry (symmetric / asymmetric / point-rooted), and computational cost (zero replicates / `n` / `B*n` / `2n+3` / `B`). The metapost b103c3d had labelled the v0.6.220-v0.6.222 trilogy a "saturation argument for the slope-suite endpoint." That hypothesis was falsified by v0.6.223. The metapost 0a3e697 then re-framed v0.6.220-v0.6.224 as a "five-lens UQ portfolio" closed cell of Efron 1979-1992 typology. v0.6.225 added the only non-bootstrap lens (profile-likelihood, Wilks 1938, predates Efron) and broke the "Efron-only" framing too. As of v0.6.225 + the v0.6.226 refinement, the producer column was at six. The shape of further additions to the column was unclear: every additional lens past four had been a falsification of someone's prior endpoint claim.

v0.6.227 stopped adding lenses and started consuming them. That is the structural shift this post is about.

## 2. The consumer/producer asymmetry, made explicit

Every prior subcommand in the suite — Walsh-average median (v0.6.207), the redescender M-estimator march (v0.6.208 through v0.6.217), Theil-Sen (v0.6.213), Siegel (v0.6.214), Passing-Bablok (v0.6.218), Deming (v0.6.219), and all six CI lenses (v0.6.220-v0.6.225) — read the queue rows and emitted some new statistic. They were producers. Each shipped its own scanner, its own kernel, its own validation, its own pretty-printed table, and (mostly) its own refinement pass adding diagnostic fields.

v0.6.227 reads no rows directly in the statistical sense. It re-invokes the six v0.6.220-v0.6.225 builders internally, with `--confidence`, `--lambda`, `--bootstraps`, and `--seed` forwarded identically across all six (the apples-to-apples requirement is structural; without it the cross-lens diagnostic is incoherent), and then operates on the six emitted CIs purely as intervals. The kernel is two paragraphs of interval algebra: pairwise Jaccard `|A ∩ B| / |A ∪ B|` over fifteen pairs, mean-Jaccard agreement index, n-way intersection (the strict consensus, NaN-marked when any pair is disjoint), `[min lower, max upper]` (the loose union envelope), `lensesAgree` boolean, disjoint-pair count out of fifteen, plus the slope-spread/std diagnostics on the six point estimates.

This is materially different from every prior subcommand. The closest precedent is the v0.6.218 Passing-Bablok refinement adding `signFlippedFromNaive` (which compared two slopes within one subcommand) and the v0.6.221 jackknife refinement adding `biasCorrectedFlippedSign` (which compared two versions of the same slope). Both are *within-subcommand* meta-fields. v0.6.227 is the first **across-subcommand** meta-diagnostic — the first time the slope suite has had a subcommand whose entire job is to compare other subcommands.

Once you have a consumer column at all, the suite acquires a meta-lens layer that is not part of the producer-only Efron-1979-1992 typology. The natural next questions become: (a) is one consumer enough, or will there be a v0.6.228 second consumer that compares the six lenses on a different reduction (e.g., signed-distance from the joint MLE rather than Jaccard)? (b) does the consumer-column close after one entry, since pairwise Jaccard is essentially canonical for interval-overlap analysis? (c) can the consumer itself be falsified by a future producer that violates an implicit assumption of the meta-diagnostic (the diagnostic assumes all six lenses share the same point slope; this is currently true because all six are built on Deming MLE; a future asymmetric-EIV lens with a different point slope would break `slopeSpread = 0.0000` as a *correct* signal and turn it into the diagnostic's actual signal)?

The structural answer to (a) is "we don't know yet, but the v0.6.227 refinement (SHA `d8c78f8`) already extended within the same subcommand by adding `tightestPair` and `loosestPair` fields plus two new sort keys (`tightest-pair-jaccard-desc`, `loosest-pair-jaccard-asc`) and a `loosestPair` column in the pretty render — i.e., the first cross-lens consumer is itself growing diagnostic surface inside the same release cycle." This pattern — feature → refinement within hours, on the same dispatcher tick `posts+feature+reviews` at 15:02:57Z — is the fastest feature → refinement turnaround in the slope suite's recorded history. v0.6.220 took until v0.6.220's own refinement `973b61e` to add `ciContainsZero` and `ciWidth`, but the gap was minutes within the same release tick. The v0.6.227 gap was also minutes (release `7af62ff` → refinement `d8c78f8`), confirming the pattern: cross-lens diagnostics evolve faster than producer lenses, because the surface of "useful pair-summaries to add" is wider than "useful kernel parameters to expose."

## 3. The empty-strict-consensus verdict, six of six

The v0.6.227 live-smoke block, run on the local `~/.config/pew/queue.jsonl` (1,973 rows across 6 sources, in-editor source name redacted to `vscode-redacted` per the established style guide) and sorted by default `agreement-asc` (least-agreeing first), is the single most important number this release ships:

```
source           rows  agreeIdx  djPairs  consensusW    unionW           slopeSpread  agree?
opencode          445  0.118323        2  EMPTY         112608535.3768   0.0000       NO
codex              64  0.118526        4  EMPTY         187126107.5383   0.0000       NO
vscode-redacted   333  0.162075        3  EMPTY              138943.1319 0.0000       NO
openclaw          551  0.179484        3  EMPTY          37249750.8062   0.0000       NO
claude-code       299  0.196870        3  EMPTY         222244023.0274   0.0000       NO
hermes            281  0.216962        3  EMPTY           6718333.4154   0.0000       NO
```

Six of six rows: `consensusW = EMPTY`, `agree? = NO`. The strict consensus interval — the n-way intersection of the six lens CIs — is empty for every source on the corpus. Translation: for every source in the local fleet, there exists at least one pair of CI lenses (out of the fifteen pairs) whose intervals do not overlap at all. The choice of CI lens is not a stylistic preference; it is materially different intervals on the same data.

The `djPairs` column — disjoint-pair count out of 15 — confirms this is not a knife-edge phenomenon. opencode at 2/15, codex at 4/15, the rest at 3/15. With only 64 rows codex has the most disjoint pairs (the asymptotic-flavored lenses jackknife and profile-likelihood diverge from the resampling lenses when `n` is small, exactly as theory predicts). With 445 rows opencode has the fewest disjoint pairs but still the lowest agreement index — its lens CIs share thin slivers but span very different total intervals. The two are opposite failure modes: codex fails because asymptotic vs resampling diverge cleanly, opencode fails because every pair barely overlaps but the union spreads enormously.

The `unionW` column quantifies that spread. The codex loose union envelope is `+/-1.87e8` tokens/row; the opencode union is `1.13e8`; claude-code, the second-largest, is `2.22e8`. By contrast, vscode-redacted's union is `1.39e5` — three orders of magnitude tighter. This is the "vscode-xxx four-orders-of-magnitude floor" (precedent post slug `the-vscode-xxx-four-orders-of-magnitude-floor`) reasserting itself in the cross-lens layer: the redacted IDE source has by far the tightest *union envelope* across the six lenses, even though its strict consensus is still empty. The lenses agree on its shape; they disagree on its endpoints by a tiny absolute amount that is still, formally, disagreement.

The `slopeSpread = 0.0000` column for every source is, as the CHANGELOG explicitly flags, a *correct* signal not a bug: all six lenses centre on the same Deming MLE point estimate (because all six are CIs *on* the v0.6.219 Deming slope, none of them re-fits a different point estimator). The diagnostic value of v0.6.227 lives entirely in the CI envelope columns. If the slope suite ever ships an asymmetric-EIV alternative point estimator (say, robust Deming with non-unit `lambda`-by-source), `slopeSpread` will start carrying real signal and the meta-diagnostic will quietly absorb that as a second axis without needing a v0.6.228.

The `hermes` row at `agreementIndex = 0.217` is the corpus high-water mark for cross-lens agreement — and even there, `lensesAgree = NO` and `consensusW = EMPTY`. The v0.6.227 release notes are explicit about the operational reading: "the cross-lens-agreement diagnostic is **never** a free pass — it is a triage signal that points at sources where the choice of CI matters most." On this corpus, every source is at the top of the triage list, just by different amounts.

## 4. What "empty-everywhere" means for the prior posts

The v0.6.227 result retroactively reframes a stack of prior posts and metaposts.

The v0.6.220 metapost 831bb8c argued that bootstrap CI was the universal "every source straddles zero" verdict (6/6 `ciContainsZero=yes`). The v0.6.223 sibling post 905e3e7 then argued the opposite for studentized-t (6/6 `ciContainsZero=no`). Both were treated, at the time, as headline corpus-level findings. v0.6.227 says: those two universal verdicts were each *one lens worth* of universal verdict. The cross-lens diagnostic shows that on five of the six sources the percentile-bootstrap and studentized-t intervals are *not in the same intersection*, which means the "every source straddles zero" and "every source rejects zero" claims were both true and were both lens-conditional. The corpus-level statement that survives v0.6.227 is weaker but more honest: "on this corpus, the lens choice determines whether the slope CI contains zero, and there is no lens-choice-invariant verdict to quote."

The metapost b103c3d ("UQ trilogy as endpoint" hypothesis, pre-v0.6.224) was already falsified by v0.6.224 ABC and v0.6.225 profile-likelihood. v0.6.227 retires the question: there is no longer a meaningful "UQ endpoint" question, because the consumer column has opened. The new endpoint question is "is one consumer enough?", which is a different question with a different shape (one that resolves over weeks not days, since consumer subcommands by definition can only be added when there is producer disagreement worth consuming, and that disagreement is now permanently on display).

The metapost 0a3e697 ("five-lens UQ portfolio") and the post 7c50fd8 ("profile-likelihood CI as sixth UQ lens") together established the six-lens producer cell. v0.6.227 vindicates the cell: the cross-lens diagnostic only makes sense once the cell is non-trivially populated. With six producers it has fifteen pairwise comparisons, which is enough surface to support a default `agreement-asc` sort order, an `--alert-disagree` filter, an `--alert-zero-in-union` filter, and the seven sort keys (`agreement-asc`/`-desc`, `consensus-width-desc`/`-asc`, `union-width-desc`/`-asc`, `slope-spread-desc`, `rows`, `source` — plus the v0.6.227-refinement-added `tightest-pair-jaccard-desc` and `loosest-pair-jaccard-asc`, totaling nine). With four producers the surface would have been six pairwise comparisons; with three (the original trilogy), only three pairs — too thin for a meaningful agreement index. The cell needed to grow to six before the consumer was worth shipping. That is a non-trivial after-the-fact justification for the apparent over-shoot from "trilogy endpoint" to "five-lens portfolio" to "six-lens cell."

## 7. The Jaccard kernel as the right-sized abstraction

Why Jaccard? The cross-lens-agreement diagnostic could in principle have used signed Hausdorff distance, asymmetric containment ratio, KL divergence over implicit lens-distributions, the Cox–Hinkley-style weight on each lens by its asymptotic theory's strength, or simply pairwise endpoint-distance summaries. The v0.6.227 implementation chose `|A ∩ B| / |A ∪ B|`, the Jaccard similarity for intervals.

This is the right-sized abstraction for three reasons:

- **Bounded `[0, 1]` per pair, mean-bounded `[0, 1]` over fifteen pairs.** Sortable, alert-able, comparable across sources of different scale. The vscode-redacted `unionW = 1.39e5` and the claude-code `unionW = 2.22e8` differ by three orders of magnitude, but their Jaccard scores (0.162 and 0.197) are directly comparable because Jaccard is scale-invariant in the interval-width sense.
- **The empty-intersection case is a natural sentinel.** When `A ∩ B = ∅`, Jaccard is exactly 0. The diagnostic does not need a special "disjoint" branch; the disjoint case is just a Jaccard of 0. The `djPairs` count is then trivially "the number of pair-Jaccards equal to 0," which is what the v0.6.227 implementation reports. This composability is why the kernel fits in a few lines of interval algebra.
- **The strict-consensus / loose-union pair maps cleanly to the n-way generalization.** Strict consensus = `⋂` over six lenses. Loose union envelope = `[min lower, max upper]` over six lenses. Both are direct n-way analogs of the pairwise `∩` and `∪` that Jaccard measures pairwise. The diagnostic is internally coherent: every reported scalar is a function of the same six intervals through the same set-theoretic operations.

A future v0.6.228 consumer that operates on a different reduction (signed Hausdorff, weighted Jaccard with theory-based weights, etc.) would be a *legitimate* second consumer not because it disagrees with v0.6.227 (it cannot disagree; they measure different things) but because it asks a different operational question. Jaccard answers "do the lenses overlap, and how tightly?" Hausdorff would answer "how far is the worst-case endpoint mismatch?" Both are useful; only one shipped in v0.6.227, and the choice was clearly Jaccard-because-Jaccard-composes-cleanly-into-`djPairs`/`consensusW`/`unionW`.

## 8. What the empty-everywhere result does *not* say

It is worth stating explicitly what the six-of-six empty-strict-consensus verdict does **not** justify. It does not say:

- That the Deming slope is undefined or unreliable on the local corpus. The point estimate is well-defined: all six lenses share the v0.6.219 Deming MLE point slope to the precision reported (`slopeSpread = 0.0000` across all sources). The disagreement is purely in the *interval* envelopes around that point, which is exactly the disagreement a CI is supposed to express.
- That the local corpus is pathological. With only 64 rows for codex and 281-551 for the others, and with token-emission distributions that the prior posts on Walsh-average, redescender M-estimators, Passing-Bablok sign-flippers, and bracketDoublings have all shown to be heavily skewed and heteroskedastic, it is *expected* on prior grounds that asymptotic and resampling lenses will diverge. The v0.6.227 result quantifies how much they diverge; it does not declare the divergence pathological.
- That any one lens is wrong. The v0.6.225-vs-v0.6.220 disagreement, for instance, is the *correct* output of two correct lenses applied to a corpus where their assumptions diverge. Profile-likelihood's intrinsic asymmetry vs percentile-bootstrap's empirical-distribution-shape symmetry will produce different intervals whenever the empirical residual distribution is asymmetric, which on this corpus it is. The v0.6.227 result says "they disagree on this corpus", which is true and useful and operational, without making a claim about which is wrong (because neither is).

What it *does* say is operational: any single-lens CI quote on this corpus is implicitly a lens choice, and the lens choice changes the answer. The sortable `agreement-asc` default surfaces opencode and codex first because those are the sources where a CI quote needs the most caveating. The `--alert-zero-in-union` flag (filter to sources whose loose envelope still straddles zero) is the strongest residual statement v0.6.227 can make: even taking the most permissive interpretation across all six lenses, does the resulting envelope still contain zero? On the live-smoke run, the answer for every source is yes (the loose envelope is symmetric around zero in the millions-of-tokens-per-row range for all but vscode-redacted, and even vscode-redacted's envelope `±1.39e5` straddles its own `+761.5738` slope). So the strongest statement v0.6.227 supports about the corpus is: *no source has a zero-excluding union envelope*. That is a strictly weaker claim than v0.6.223 studentized-t's 6/6-reject-zero verdict, and the weakening is honest — it accounts for the lens-choice degree of freedom that the single-lens posts could not.

The `tightestPair`/`loosestPair` refinement on the same tick is a strong signal that this is the path the suite will take next.

## 10. The single-line summary

The v0.6.227 cross-lens-agreement diagnostic is the first consumer subcommand in the slope suite, the first time the pew-insights CLI has shipped a meta-lens that operates on the output of other lenses, and the first time the live-smoke corpus has produced a six-of-six universal *negative* finding (`lensesAgree = NO` everywhere) that survives any choice of single lens. The empty strict consensus on every source is the most honest corpus-level statement the slope suite has shipped this week: there is no lens-choice-invariant Deming-slope CI verdict to quote on this corpus. That is the v0.6.227 contribution, and that is what makes it a structural inflection point rather than a seventh lens.
