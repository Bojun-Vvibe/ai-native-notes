A naive read of synth #375 alone would describe litellm as "running hot." A naive read of synth #376 alone would describe codex as "running cold." ## The asymmetry is not symmetric

It is tempting to read synth #375 and #376 as duals — one says "litellm goes up," one says "codex goes down" — but they are not duals along the same axis. Synth #375 is a per-tick over-recovery shape that names a NEW W17 shape (direct amplifying back-to-back, no silence separator). Synth #376 is a multi-tick suppression-band that names an extended sub-floor flat tail. The over-recovery shape lives at the upper edge of the per-tick rate distribution; the suppression band lives at the lower edge of the per-tick PER-REPO rate distribution. The cross-repo aggregate at Add.173 was 8 merges at 0.2122/min — upper band — driven by the litellm septuple. Per-repo the codex contribution to that 0.2122/min was effectively the floor (1 / 8 = 12.5% of cross-repo count, vs codex's typical 25-40% share at non-suppression ticks). So the asymmetry is: the cross-repo aggregate is being held in the upper rate band by a single repo (litellm) while a different single repo (codex) is being held in a multi-tick floor that is structurally invariant across changes in the cross-repo aggregate. Codex's suppression is not caused by low cross-repo activity; it persists despite cross-repo activity being at the upper band.

This is structurally distinct from the M-168.A "over-recovery as dominant tick-level regime" framing. M-168.A says the cross-repo aggregate is being driven by intra-repo over-recovery instances. Synth #376 says one repo (codex) is decoupled from that regime entirely — it neither participates in the over-recovery side nor reverts to its historical mid-band emission. The decoupling is the thing that makes the synth pair more than the sum of its parts. If the M-168.A regime were uniform across repos you'd expect codex to participate as the originator (Add.168 sextuple). Instead the originator is the most decoupled — six consecutive sub-floor ticks (5 / 4 / 6 / 1 / 1 / 1) anchored at the burst rather than oscillating around it. The post-burst suppression band is a refractory-period-after-burst hypothesis, and synth #376 is a candidate refractory-mechanism naming.
