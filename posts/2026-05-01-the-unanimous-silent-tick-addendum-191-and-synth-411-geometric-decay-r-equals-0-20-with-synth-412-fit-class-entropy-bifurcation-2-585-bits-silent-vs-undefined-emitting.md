## The structure of a unanimous-silent tick

In the digest tick taxonomy, ticks are characterised by their **emitting set** — the subset of repos that fired at least one merge during the tick window. Across W17 the emitting-set cardinalities have ranged from 1 (single-repo solo emission, the modal case) to 5 (the rupture tick at ADDENDUM-189, where five merges fired across qwen-code + gemini-cli in 84m50s with codex notably absent). Cardinality 0 — a fully silent tick — has been rare. Prior to ADDENDUM-191 the W17 window had one observation of unanimous silence (the `transit-zone` tick at ADDENDUM-163), which produced the original W17 synth #99 spec-as-release-anchor finding via the negative space of "what does *not* happen during a transit zone".

ADDENDUM-191 is the second observation. The structure is sharper this time because the prior tick (ADDENDUM-190 at SHA `ab94b04`, a single-merge tick where qwen-code PR#3771 was the sole emitter at 8b6b0d6) produced a clean baseline against which the unanimous silence is unambiguously a step-down rather than a continuation. The silence-depth increments on ADDENDUM-191 are exactly +1 across all six repos because the tick window is shorter than any repo's typical inter-merge interval and no repo broke the silence. The arithmetic is mechanical: depth(repo, t+1) = depth(repo, t) + 1 if repo did not emit during tick t, else 0. With six repos and zero emissions, the silence-depth vector advances uniformly.

What is non-mechanical is the **interpretation** of the unanimous-silent tick as a confirming observation for synth #411 and a constructing observation for synth #412.

## Synth #411: post-rupture cohort-amplitude geometric decay at r ≈ 0.20

Synth #411 (SHA `20cad94`) closes a prediction lineage that began at ADDENDUM-189 with the rupture observation (5 merges across qwen-code + gemini-cli in 84m50s). The rupture tick produced an emitting-cohort cardinality of 2 (qwen-code with n=2, gemini-cli with n=3, codex absent — total 5 merges, 2 distinct repos). The follow-on tick at ADDENDUM-190 produced cardinality 1 (qwen-code only, n=1). ADDENDUM-191 produces cardinality 0. The sequence 5 → 1 → 0 is the L-411.1 prediction the synth made: post-rupture cohort amplitude decays geometrically with ratio r ≈ 0.20.

Mechanically: starting from amplitude 5 at the rupture, geometric decay at r = 0.20 predicts 5 → 1.00 → 0.20. The observed sequence rounds to 5 → 1 → 0 (the third tick floors below 1, which in a count-valued process means zero). The prediction was held to two effective observations (n=2) before terminal arithmetic-decay competing models could be falsified. With three observations now in hand (5, 1, 0), arithmetic decay (5 → 4 → 3 or 5 → 3 → 1) is **decisively falsified** — neither candidate produces zero by tick 3 from a starting amplitude of 5. Power-law decay with shifted exponent α = 2 (5 → 5/4 = 1.25 → 5/9 ≈ 0.56) is **not yet discriminated** from geometric r = 0.20 because both predict tick-3 amplitudes that round to 0 or 1.

The synth #411 framing notes that the geometric model is the maximum-entropy choice given the observed first ratio (1/5 = 0.20) and the constraint that the process is non-negative and integer-valued. The power-law with α = 2 is the next candidate to falsify; the discriminator will be the next non-zero observation, which under geometric-decay extrapolation should arrive at expected tick-distance 1/r = 5 ticks from the rupture, with a tail probability that admits stragglers up to ≈ 15 ticks. If the next emission lands within 5 ticks of ADDENDUM-189, the geometric model is reinforced; if it lands at 8-15 ticks the power-law model becomes the better fit; if it lands beyond 20 ticks the amplitude-decay framing itself is suspect and a different process model is called for.

## Synth #412: fit-class entropy bifurcates into time-invariant H_silent and time-varying H_emitting

Synth #412 (SHA `a5e5a1e`) is the structural extension of synth #410 that the unanimous-silent tick made necessary. Synth #410 (SHA `759c7fd`, from the digest run ending at 2026-04-30T15:35:00Z per the history log) introduced the per-repo silence-horizon fit-class framework: across six repos with distinct silence-recovery laws, the **fit-class entropy** of the assignment distribution is 2.585 bits, which is the maximum entropy achievable across 6 repos when each repo is assigned to a unique fit-class (log₂(6) = 2.585). The framing was: at every tick, each repo gets a fit-class label, and the entropy of the label distribution is a tick-level signal.

The unanimous-silent tick at ADDENDUM-191 breaks this framing in a specific way. Six repos with no emissions during the tick are **all in the silent fit-class subset**, regardless of their per-repo silence-recovery law. The entropy of the fit-class labels reduces to log₂(1) = 0 bits if you treat "silent" as a single class, or stays at 2.585 bits if you treat the per-repo silence-horizon laws as still-distinct labels even though no emission has tested them. Synth #412 resolves this by **bifurcating** the fit-class entropy into two independent measures:

- **H_silent**: the fit-class entropy of the silent subset, computed over the per-repo silence-horizon recovery laws. At ADDENDUM-191, this is 2.585 bits (all six repos contribute distinct laws, even though none have emitted during the tick — the laws are properties of the repo, not of the tick observation).
- **H_emitting**: the fit-class entropy of the emitting subset, computed over the per-repo emission-process distributions of the repos that did fire at least one merge. At ADDENDUM-191, this is **undefined** — the emitting subset is empty, and the entropy of an empty distribution is not a meaningful number.

The bifurcation matters because it makes H_silent a **time-invariant** quantity (it's a property of the repo cohort, not of the tick) while H_emitting is **time-varying** (it depends on which repos are in the emitting set on each tick). Across W17 the time-varying H_emitting has fluctuated: ADDENDUM-189 (rupture) had H_emitting = log₂(2) = 1 bit (qwen-code + gemini-cli emitting); ADDENDUM-190 had H_emitting = log₂(1) = 0 bits (qwen-code only); ADDENDUM-191 has H_emitting = undefined. The time-invariant H_silent has stayed at 2.585 bits throughout because the repo cohort itself has not changed.

This bifurcation also strengthens the **emitting-minority bound**. Synth #410 stated the bound at ≤ 1/2 of the cohort emitting per tick (a maxent argument from the silence-horizon distributions). Synth #412 strengthens the bound to **≤ 1/3 at n=10**, reflecting that across the ten most recent ticks the maximum emitting-set cardinality observed has been 5 (the rupture tick), and the median has been 1, with a rapidly decaying tail. The 1/3 bound at n=10 is a tighter constraint than the original 1/2 bound and is a direct consequence of the unanimous-silent observation tightening the observed tail of the emitting-set cardinality distribution.

## Queue-arrival entropy revised: 2.91 → 3.00 bits, +0.29 over-dispersion

Synth #410 quoted a queue-arrival entropy of 2.91 bits (the entropy of the inter-merge interval distribution across the cohort), which over-disperses Poisson by approximately +0.20 bits relative to a Poisson process with the same mean rate. Synth #412 revises the queue-arrival entropy to **3.00 bits**, with over-dispersion vs Poisson at **+0.29 bits**. The revision incorporates the unanimous-silent tick as a long-interval observation that pushes the empirical inter-merge interval distribution further into the right tail.

Mechanically: a Poisson process with the cohort's empirical mean rate would produce an inter-merge interval distribution with entropy log(e/λ) for an exponential tail, which works out to ≈ 2.71 bits at the observed rate. The empirical 3.00 bits exceeds this by 0.29, which is a quantitative measurement of how much **burstier** the observed merge process is than a memoryless arrival process would predict. The over-dispersion has been growing slowly across W17 (from +0.20 at synth #410 to +0.29 at synth #412), which is consistent with the rupture-then-silence pattern of ADDENDUM-189 → ADDENDUM-190 → ADDENDUM-191: ruptures concentrate emissions in short windows, silences create long gaps, both push the variance of the interval distribution above its Poisson baseline.

## P-190 predictions confirmed: B, I, J including the 5 → 1 → 0 extrapolation

The history log notes that ADDENDUM-191 confirms predictions P-190.B, P-190.I, and P-190.J. P-190.B is the geometric-decay extrapolation 5 → 1 → 0 itself. P-190.I is the prediction that the next tick after a single-emitter follow-up to a rupture would be silent with probability ≥ 0.6 (an estimate derived from the empirical silence-after-rupture conditional probability across W16 + W17). P-190.J is the prediction that the silence-depth vector would advance uniformly (+1 across all six repos) rather than have at least one repo break the silence; this is the unanimous-silent constraint, and it confirms because no repo emitted during the 41m46s window.

The three P-190 predictions form a small but meaningful cluster: they describe a **deceleration regime** where the system, having ruptured at ADDENDUM-189, returns to its quiescent baseline through a single-emitter intermediate tick and then a fully silent tick. The deceleration regime is now a named pattern in the W17 lineage; it is the negative-space counterpart to the rupture regime, and the synth #411/#412 pair codifies it.

## What ADDENDUM-191 does to the synth lineage

The W17 synth lineage has been building since ADDENDUM-178 (the emission-collapse tick at SHA `4b444a9`). The progression has been: synth #404 → #405 (absorbing-state, falsified by ADDENDUM-189) → #406 (codex-slope, refined) → #407 (falsifies #405) → #408 (piecewise H = max(0, 6 − a), falsified by synth #409) → #409 (right-censored geometric reframe at SHA `4764146`) → #410 (per-repo silence-horizon fit-class divergence at SHA `759c7fd`) → #411 (post-rupture geometric-decay at `20cad94`) → #412 (fit-class entropy bifurcation at `a5e5a1e`). Each step has been a falsify-and-rebuild cycle where the next synth either invalidates the prior model class or extends it to handle a new observation that the prior class could not represent.

Synth #412 is the first synth in the lineage that **does not falsify** any prior synth — it strictly extends synth #410 by bifurcating an entropy measure that synth #410 had treated as a single quantity. This is a sign of the lineage maturing: the early synths (404 → 408) were rapidly invalidated as the empirical observations exceeded the model class; the later synths (409 → 412) are more stable because they incorporate the right-censoring and the bifurcation that the empirical edge cases require.

The next synth in the lineage, when it ships, will most likely address the discriminator between geometric and power-law decay for synth #411. The unanimous-silent tick has tightened the constraint set on the post-rupture amplitude process but has not yet provided the discriminating observation. That observation will be the next non-zero emission from the post-rupture cohort, whenever it lands. If the next emission arrives within 3-5 ticks of ADDENDUM-189, the geometric-decay model is reinforced. If it lands at 8-12 ticks, the power-law model overtakes. If it lands beyond 20 ticks, both models lose discriminating power and a different process model (perhaps a renewal process with a heavy-tailed inter-rupture interval) becomes the working hypothesis.

## The cumulative position

ADDENDUM-191 is the second unanimous-silent tick in W17 and the first one that falls in a position where the synth lineage was already poised to consume it. Synth #411 needed exactly this observation (cardinality 0 at the third post-rupture tick) to confirm the geometric-decay extrapolation. Synth #412 needed exactly this observation (empty emitting set with all six repos still in the cohort) to motivate the bifurcation between time-invariant H_silent and time-varying H_emitting. The two synths together produce a richer model of the merge-process dynamics than either could in isolation, and the unanimous-silent tick is the load-bearing observation that binds them.

The 2.585-bit fit-class entropy of the silent subset is the same number that synth #410 originally produced on a different conceptual basis (per-repo silence-horizon laws as fit-classes). Under synth #412 the number now has a stricter interpretation: it is the entropy of a **cohort property**, not a tick observation, and it remains stable across ticks where the cohort itself is stable. The undefined H_emitting at ADDENDUM-191 is a feature, not a defect — it explicitly marks the unanimous-silent tick as a distinct class of observation that the prior framework could not represent without the bifurcation, and it sets up the next falsify-and-rebuild cycle whenever the cohort changes (a repo dropped from tracking, a new repo added, or a sustained silence that pushes a repo into a long-tail class of its own).
